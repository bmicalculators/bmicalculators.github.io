What is BMI?

Understanding Body Mass Index

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a simple but important measurement used to assess whether a person has a healthy body weight for their height. It is a value derived from an individual's weight and height.

BMI Categories

  • Underweight Below 18.5
  • Normal Weight 18.5 - 24.9
  • Overweight 25 - 29.9
  • Obese 30 or greater

How is BMI Calculated?

BMI is calculated using the following formulas:

Metric Formula

BMI = weight (kg) / height² (m²)

Imperial Formula

BMI = (weight (lbs) × 703) / height² (inches²)

Why Gender Matters in BMI

BMI interpretation can vary between men and women due to different body compositions:

  • Men typically have more muscle mass
  • Women naturally have higher body fat percentages
  • Body fat distribution patterns differ between genders

Limitations of BMI

While BMI is a useful screening tool, it has some limitations:

  • Doesn't account for muscle mass
  • May not be suitable for athletes
  • Doesn't measure body fat distribution
  • May not be appropriate for elderly individuals
